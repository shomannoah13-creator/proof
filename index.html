Built from real issues observed in active legal practice.

The Problem

AI-generated legal work can look credible before it is actually supportable.

Real citations. Confident language. But the reasoning has already moved outside what the source permits.

The risk isn't obvious errors. It's the moment confidence becomes reliance — before anyone has verified the reasoning holds up.

What Proof Verifies

Not just citations. The reasoning behind them.

Whether cited sources actually support the claim — not just whether the citation exists, but whether it says what the argument claims it says.

Whether arguments overstate or misapply authority — catching cases where a source is real but used beyond its actual holding.

Whether the reasoning stays within the limits of the source — identifying when an AI draws conclusions the source does not support.

What Proof Is Not

Built for one thing only.

Not a legal research tool — Proof does not find cases or statutes for you.

Not a drafting assistant — Proof does not write arguments or generate legal content.

Not another AI generator — Proof sits at the end of the workflow, not the beginning.

Proof is the verification layer before reliance — the control point between AI output and trusted legal reasoning.
