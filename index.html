The Problem

AI-generated legal work can look correct even when it isn't.

Real citations. Confident language. But the reasoning doesn't hold up.

The risk isn't obvious errors. It's when sources don't actually support the argument being made.

What Proof Checks

Not just citations. The reasoning behind them.

Whether cited sources actually support the claim — not just whether the citation exists, but whether it says what the argument claims it says.

Whether arguments overstate or misapply authority — catching cases where a source is real but used beyond its actual holding.

Whether reasoning stays within what the source says — identifying when an AI draws conclusions the source does not support.
